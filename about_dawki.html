About Dawki

Dawki is a small town located in the West Jaintia Hills district of Meghalaya, India. It is situated on the border with Bangladesh and is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. One of the main attractions in Dawki is the Umngot River, which flows through the town and forms the India-Bangladesh border. The river is crystal clear, and its waters are so transparent that you can see fish swimming in them from a distance. The river is also a popular spot for boating and fishing. Dawki is also home to a number of scenic viewpoints, waterfalls, and trekking trails, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The town has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Khasi and Bengali influences. The local people are known for their warm hospitality and traditional cuisine, which includes dishes such as jadoh (a rice and meat dish) and tungtap (a type of fermented fish). In recent years, Dawki has gained popularity as a tourist destination, particularly among those interested in eco-tourism and cultural tourism. The town has a number of eco-lodges and homestays, which offer comfortable accommodation and the opportunity to experience the local culture and way of life. Overall, Dawki is a hidden gem in the heart of Meghalaya, offering a unique and unforgettable experience for those who visit.

Other popular attractions near Dawki include:

  • - Dawki Bridge: A suspension bridge over the Umngot River that offers stunning views.
  • - Jaflong: A scenic spot known for its waterfalls and trekking trails.
  • - Krang Suri Waterfall: A beautiful waterfall located a short drive from Dawki.
  • - Mawlynnong Village: A nearby village known for its cleanliness and natural beauty.
